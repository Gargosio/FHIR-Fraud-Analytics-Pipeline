# Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) – Based Fraud Detection Analytics Models
The demonstration dashboard is built using Synthea FHIR data. The dataset is synthetic (completely fictional but realistic) patient health records generated by Synthea, an open-source synthetic patient population simulator developed by MITRE Corporation. Synthea FHIR data is a privacy-safe, standardized, synthetic version of real-world electronic health records, widely used as a stand-in for actual patient data in development and research.
FHIR is a global standard that seeks to foster secure interoperability between various medical systems through exchanging electronic health information through standardized data packets referred to as ‘Resources’. Kenya, through Digital Health Agency (DHA), has adopted the use of the FHIR standard in SHA, allowing health information exchange between all medical facilities. The key resources used in this fraud detection demo model are: Encounter, Observation, DiagnosticReport, MedicationRequest & Claim, samples attached in this document’s annex. 
The dashboard is a true depiction of what happens in a typical hospital where a patient visits the medical facility to explain to the practitioner his symptoms (Encounter). The practitioner then recommends relevant lab tests depending on the described symptoms (Observation). The lab results are then reviewed by the practitioner who gives a diagnosis (DiagnosticReport), prescribes medications based on the diagnosis (MedicationRequest). The full encounter is later billed and a claim issued to the patient’s insurer (Claim).
 The dataset used is denormalized view joining encounters, observations, patients, medications, diagnosis and claims) contains key elements typical of FHIR-style or claims/encounter data: patient identifiers, encounter timing and type, practitioner/service provider, clinical observations (e.g., labs/vitals),diagnoses reports medication orders, and billing/claim details (including value, status, billable period, product/service).This structure allows detection of many common healthcare fraud schemes, particularly provider-side billing fraud (which accounts for the majority of detected cases in claims data).

Below are the most relevant fraudulent schemes that can realistically be flagged or investigated using this data, grouped by category, with explanations of how the available columns support detection.
1.	Phantom Billing by Product: Claims with Minimal/No Clinical Evidence (Observations, Diagnosis & Medication)
2.	Short Duration Encounters with Claims : Encounters whose duration is less than 3 minutes but have an accompanying claim
4.	Phantom Billing by Facility: Claims with Minimal/No Clinical Evidence (Observations, Diagnosis & Medication)
5.	Up coding: High-Value Claims with Low/minimal Clinical Justification
6.	Potential Unbundling: Multiple Claims per Encounter
7.	Over-Prescription: Medication Fraud. Flags meds without matching diagnosis
